## What This Solves

Teams often want to use AI inside VP work, but the real question is not whether AI is available. The real question is which tasks benefit from it without creating ambiguity or lowering production discipline.

## Good Use Cases

### Convert Notes into Action Lists

After a review session, AI can turn raw comments into grouped action items such as layout changes, camera adjustments, asset needs, lighting changes, and naming fixes.

### Compare Workflow Options

When the team is unsure whether to solve a problem in Unreal Engine or Blender, AI can help compare tradeoffs, surface likely risks, and suggest what to validate first.

### Draft Checklists and Naming Rules

AI is well suited to producing first-pass checklists for shot review, scene handoff, and snapshot packaging. These still need human revision, but they reduce blank-page work.

## Weak Use Cases

### Final Visual Judgment

AI cannot replace the review of framing, readability, depth, or the emotional logic of a shot.

### Ownership Decisions

Who should touch the scene next, whether a shot is stable enough to package, and whether an asset is production-ready should remain team decisions.

## A Practical Decision Rule

Use AI when the task is one of these:

1. Sorting
2. Summarizing
3. Comparing
4. Drafting

Do not rely on AI when the task requires one of these:

1. Spatial judgment
2. Final artistic intent
3. Ownership assignment
4. Production sign-off

## Team Habit to Build

Each time AI is used, convert the output into one of these concrete UE-facing artifacts:

- A shot checklist
- A naming standard reminder
- A categorized review note
- A next-step action list

That rule keeps AI attached to actual workflow progress instead of vague ideation.
