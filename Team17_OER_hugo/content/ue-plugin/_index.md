{{< lead >}}
This section is written for students who learn by doing: short notes on **modules and plugins**, then a **repeatable dev loop** you can adapt when you build your own tools. Treat it as a lab companion—your experiments and mistakes will teach you more than any checklist.
{{< /lead >}}

## Learning Outcome

By the end of this section, learners should be able to explain how **modules** and **plugins** relate, follow a sensible **plugin development workflow** in the editor, and use **Visual Studio** with **Live Coding** without getting stuck in endless full rebuilds—or know when a full rebuild is actually required.

## This Section Covers

1. **Foundation:** what UE **modules** and **plugins** are, and how they work together ([UE Modules & Plugins](prereq-kit/prereq-kit/)).
2. **Plugin development workflow:** goals, creating and enabling a plugin, iteration, testing, and lightweight troubleshooting ([Plugin Development Workflow](plugin-dev/plugin-development-workflow/)).
3. **Visual Studio & Live Coding:** solution setup, build configurations, when Live Coding helps, and when to stop and do a full compile ([Visual Studio & Live Coding with Unreal](plugin-dev/visual-studio-and-live-coding/)).

## How to Use This Section

Read **Prerequisite** first if Unreal’s file layout still feels unfamiliar. Then work through the **Plugin Development** series in order—the second article assumes you are already opening the `.sln` and editing module code. If something fails, skim the troubleshooting tables and the Output Log before changing ten things at once; that habit alone saves hours.
